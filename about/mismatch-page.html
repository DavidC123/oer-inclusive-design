mismatch

Medically, disability is defined as a permanent and limiting trait, that occurs when a person deviates from the norm. The views of inclusive design are different: rather than being a trait a disability it is a mismatch between our needs and the design. For example, if a child with ADHD is not focusing in a slightly challenging but boring classroom, their ADHD would take the initial blame. However, the real issue lies in the unsuitable environment. With this viewpoint, it becomes the responsibility of the designer to solve the mismatch. This perspective allows us to see that, through design, the mismatch is solvable. This is also known as the social model of disability, where limits lie in social norms rather than disabilities. Mismatches can be a result of many factors such as: context, environment, learning styles, unique personal needs and hardware and software variations.
